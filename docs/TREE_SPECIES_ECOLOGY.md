# Tree Species Ecology Guide

## Purpose

Help students choose ecologically suitable species and understand long-term impact.

## Regional Planting Guidance

- Himalayan:
  - Prefer cold-resilient species with slope-soil compatibility.
  - Monitor frost stress in first winter.
- Coastal:
  - Select salt-tolerant and wind-resilient species.
  - Ensure drainage to prevent root rot.
- Desert:
  - Prioritize low-water native species and mulching.
  - Protect saplings from heat shock and grazing.
- Forest:
  - Use mixed-species planting to increase resilience.
  - Avoid monoculture patches.
- Urban:
  - Choose pollution-tolerant species with root-safe spacing.
  - Coordinate with local civic constraints and footpaths.

## Student Care Checklist

- Week 1-4: regular watering and shade protection
- Month 2-3: staking checks and weed removal
- Month 6: canopy and stem health review
- Month 12: survival assessment and growth notes

## Health Status Definitions

- Thriving: strong growth, healthy leaves, no visible stress
- Healthy: stable growth, minor stress signs
- Struggling: reduced growth, leaf yellowing or pest signs
- Dead: no recovery signs, dry/brittle stem

## Learning Outcome Link

Tree verification teaches:
- biodiversity awareness
- ecological stewardship over time
- evidence-based reporting
- accountability in climate actions
