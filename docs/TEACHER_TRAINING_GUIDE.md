## 👥 Managing Your Students (10 minutes)

### Viewing Student Progress
1. Click **"Students"** in the sidebar
2. See a list of all your students with:
   - Name, Grade, Roll Number
   - Total Eco-Points earned
   - Engagement level (%)
   - Number of activities completed
   - Consent status (✅ or ⏳)

### Finding Struggling Students
Students are marked as **"At Risk"** if they:
- Haven't participated in 7+ days
- Fall below the class average engagement
- Have low quiz scores

**What to do:**
- Click the name to view their profile
- See what activities/quizzes they've missed
- Send them a personalized message: "Hi Arjun, I noticed you haven't submitted any activities this week. Would you like help?"

### Student Profiles
Click on any student name to see:
- Current level and badges earned
- All completed activities (with photos)
- Quiz performance
- Environmental impact (how many trees they've helped plant, etc.)
- Birthday & contact information

## ✅ Approving Student Activities (5 minutes)

Activities are how students earn eco-points. Your job: **Verify they're genuine**.

### What to Expect
Students submit photos of:
- Tree planting (show tree, student, date)
- Waste segregation (bins with labels)
- Water conservation (installing taps, using buckets)
- Community cleanups (before/after photos)

### Approval Workflow

**Step 1: Go to "Submissions"**
- Click **"Pending Approvals"** → shows unreviewed activities
- See: student name, activity type, photo, timestamp

**Step 2: Review the Photo**
- Does it show the activity?
- Is the student visible?
- Looks genuine (not stolen from internet)?
- ✅ **Approve it** → Student gets eco-points instantly
- ❌ **Reject it** → Student gets feedback to resubmit

**Step 3: Optional Feedback**
- Add comment: "Great work! More details next time."
- Student receives email with feedback

### ⚡ What is AI Pre-Verification?
EcoKids uses AI to automatically check photos for:
- Whether the photo actually shows the claimed activity
- Duplicate/fake submissions
- Safety concerns

**AI marks submissions as:**
- 🟢 **Likely Genuine** (90%+ confidence) → Auto-approved
- 🟡 **Needs Review** (50-90%) → You decide
- 🔴 **Likely Fake** (< 50%) → Automatically rejected

Your manual review overrides AI decisions!

## 🧠 Running a Classroom Quiz (5 minutes)

Quizzes teach environmental concepts. You can:
- Use **pre-made quizzes** (created by EcoKids experts, aligned to NEP 2020)
- **Create your own** (with AI help)
- View results immediately

### Using a Pre-Made Quiz

1. Click **"Quizzes"** in sidebar
2. Choose: Grade level → Topic (e.g., "Renewable Energy") → Quiz
3. Click **"Assign to Class"**
4. Students get notification and can take the quiz
5. Results appear in real-time as they submit

### Creating a Custom Quiz with AI

1. Click **"Create Quiz"**
2. Type: "Create a 5-question quiz on plastic pollution for Grade 7"
3. Click **"Generate with AI"**
4. Review questions, edit if needed
5. Click **"Publish"**
6. Immediately assign to your class

### Viewing Results
- % of class that passed
- Students with < 60% → Flag for extra help
- Download results as Excel file for parent reports

## 🎯 Setting Weekly Missions (5 minutes)

**Missions** are mini-challenges that motivate students for the week.

### Examples
- "Plant 5 trees by Friday" (eco-points reward)
- "Reduce plastic use: bring a reusable water bottle for 3 days"
- "Quiz Challenge: Score ≥80% on renewable energy quiz"

### How to Create

1. Click **"Missions"** → **"Create New"**
2. Enter:
   - **Mission name**: "Zero-Waste Lunch Week"
   - **Description**: "Bring zero-plastic lunch 3 days this week"
   - **Due date**: Friday, 3:00 PM
   - **Reward**: 50 eco-points
3. **Choose grade levels** this applies to
4. Click **"Publish"**
5. Students see it on their dashboard with a countdown timer

### Why Missions Work
- Students **see clear goals** (not just "do more activities")
- Families get involved ("Mom, let's make a zero-waste lunch!")
- Weekly themes connect to curriculum
- Competitive element: "Our class got 500 points this week!"

### Linking to Curriculum
**📚 Expert tip:** Align missions to what you're teaching:
- Teaching about **water pollution**? → Mission: "Research 3 ways to reduce water waste"
- Teaching **biodiversity**? → Mission: "Observe 5 different plants/animals in nature"
- Teaching **climate**? → Mission: "Calculate your carbon footprint"

## 🎁 Common Questions (FAQ)

### Q: A student says "I earned eco-points but they're not in my account"
**A:** Usually the activity wasn't **approved yet**. Go to Submissions → check if it's pending. Also check if they met the photo requirements (clear, includes student, date stamp preferred).

### Q: Can I use EcoKids during class time?
**A:** **Yes!** EcoKids works best as:
- Opening activity (2 min): "Today's quiz challenge is..."
- Mid-class: Submit an activity while you supervise
- Closing: Check leaderboard, celebrate top student
- Never just "screen time" — always discuss the environmental concept

### Q: Parent asked: "How do I see my child's progress?"
**A:** Tell parent:
1. Go to EcoKids main site
2. Click **"Parent Report"**
3. Enter child's name + school code
4. See: eco-points, badges, activities completed, quiz scores
5. Or download monthly report (you provide)

### Q: One student has zero eco-points but says they did activities
**A:** Check:
1. Activities aren't **submitted** (they're in draft)
2. Activities are **pending approval** (you haven't reviewed yet)
3. Activities were **rejected** (photo didn't show activity clearly)
4. Have them resubmit with clearer photos

### Q: I want to add a new student mid-year
**A:** Go to **"Students"** → **"Add Student"**
- Enter name, grade, roll number
- They get auto-added to your class
- They can login with email + school code

### Q: How do I track if my class meets NEP goals?
**A:** Click **"Analytics"** → **"NEP Competency"**
- Shows % meeting each of 6 competencies
- Which students need help with each
- Recommended activities/quizzes for that competency
- Export report for principal

### Q: Can students work in groups for activities?
**A:** Yes! Go to **"Create Activity"** → toggle **"Allow Group Submission"**
- Multiple students can submit together
- All get equal eco-points
- Great for "plant a tree" or "community cleanup"

### Q: How often should I send missions?
**A:** Recommended: **1 mission per week** (Monday morning)
- Gives students 5 days to complete
- Keeps momentum without overwhelming
- Seasonal missions during holidays

## 💪 Best Practices

### Build Classroom Culture
- **Celebrate progress**: "Congratulations! Grade 8-A hit 1000 eco-points this month!"
- **Highlight effort, not just top scorers**: "Arjun improved from 20 to 60% quiz scores — great improvement!"
- **Connect to real impact**: "Your class planted 47 trees this week = 1 tonne of CO₂ prevented!"

### Integrate with Curriculum
- Assign **quiz** → teach concept → students submit **activity evidence**
- Example: Day 1: Teach "plastic pollution" → Day 2: Quiz → Day 3-5: Submit cleanup photos

### Differentiation
- **Low engagement students**: Assign easier missions, one-on-one check-ins
- **High performers**: Challenge missions ("Design a zero-waste lunch plan for the school")
- **Recent joiners**: Give extra approval feedback to build confidence

### Parent Communication
- Send **weekly email** highlighting class achievements
- Include **screenshot** of top activities
- Explain **how to access parent report** (many don't know it exists)
